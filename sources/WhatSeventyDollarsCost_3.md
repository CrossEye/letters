Congratulations, Andover No voters, you saved a little money.  But have you considered the costs?

First the figures.  Between the first budget and the last, the school budget was reduced by $224,261.  Chop off $5,000 for the costs of running two referendums. Divide the result by Andover's population of 3,150, and you get $69.61.

That's what you saved.  Seventy bucks.  Congratulations.  I hope you feel good about yourself.

So at whose expense did you achieve these phenomenal savings?  Simple: the schoolchildren.  Here's what the BOE had to cut to give you this windfall:

  * Two paraprofessional school aides.  They're gone.  No more.  The support they offered the kids?  Gone.  

  * The Spanish program.  Were the kids reading Cervantes when they reached RHAM?  Perhaps not.  But they were exposed to the second most common language spoken in our country.  No more.  Adiós, Spanish.

  * Stipends for teachers who want to go beyond with the kids: Curriculum work. Robotics League.  Yearbook advisor.  Gone, unless the school can conjure up grants for the teachers to do this work after hours.

  * The library.  Yes, the library.  They've eliminated the position of librarian/media specialist.

Let me say that one more time.  They eliminated the library, the most sacred place in any school.  You cut our kids' school library.

Let me tell you about Mrs. Hatch.  At Great Plains Elementary School in the early '70s, like many other kids, I knew that the best part of school was the playground. But only slightly behind this was this mystical, magical room called The Library.  Mrs. Hatch ruled the roost there for my whole elementary school. She read stories to the youngsters and taught us as we were older how to read them to the little ones.  She explained how to research, to branch out and try new sorts of literature.  She taught us how to properly care for books, and to never, ever abuse them.  She taught at least one little boy a love for reading that lasts until this day.  Mrs. Hatch may have only been 50 years old, but to a third-grader, that was as old as the hills, with the wisdome to match.

You've decided that our schoolchildren don't need their own Mrs. Hatch.  I'm sorry, but you're terribly wrong.  In this modern, much more rushed world, there's little that the kids need more than a love of reading.

So enjoy your $70.  Spend it wisely.  But when you do, spare a thought for the full costs.
