Title: Won't Get Fooled Again

Twice now, Andover's First Selectman has mailed every household a flyer urging a
No vote on the school budget. Both times the mailing landed after the
Rivereast's deadline, too late for any correction to reach print before we
voted.   Twice is not a coincidence.  It is a method: make the scary claim, and
arrange things so no one can answer it in time.

So let me answer it now, before the next one arrives.

Both flyers were built on numbers designed to alarm, and both fell apart the
moment anyone checked.  The first implied the preschool drains the town's
budget; in fact it saves us over $100,000 a year.  The second implied that
adding one student costs the town more than $81,000, a figure no honest reading
of the numbers supports: treating the average cost of running the whole school
as if it were the cost of one more child.  I documented every correction, with
the town's own audits and the state's own data, at andoverct.info.  The flyers
did not link to their sources.  My site does.

Here is something worth remembering.  Our First Selectman reminds us, often,
that he is a CPA.  The accounting profession's own code of conduct asks its
members to "serve the public interest, honor the public trust, and demonstrate a
commitment to professionalism."  Mailings engineered to mislead, and timed so
the truth can't catch up, honor none of those things.
(pub.aicpa.org/codeofconduct/ethicsresources/et-cod.pdf#page=15)

Now the budget itself.  After last year's cuts and this year's failed votes,
what remains is a 6% increase.  Even at 6%, the school must either cut programs
(library? art? music?) or keep some grades at 25 to 30 students in a single
classroom.  That is the real choice in front of us.  Not a hidden fortune, not a
runaway school.  A lean budget, and class sizes that are already too large.

We have also seen, more than once, how this First Selectman treats the residents
who dare to disagree with him in public.  That is not how a town of neighbors
should speak to itself.

So if a third flyer turns up this weekend, full of frightening figures and
conveniently impossible to answer before Tuesday, you will know exactly what it
is.  You have seen this movie twice.

Vote yes on June 16.  This time, we won't get fooled again!
