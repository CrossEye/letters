You should know what your representative did on May 6.

It was the last day of the legislative session. Senate Bill 274 was on the
floor. The bill would have done two things: increased penalties when landlords
rack up repeat fire and building code violations, and required non-resident
apartment owners to register with the towns where their buildings sit. It was a
direct response to the Concierge Apartments disaster in Rocky Hill, where an
out-of-state private equity firm let a complex deteriorate until thousands of
tenants had to be put up in hotels at public expense. The bill had passed the
Senate. It had cleared the Planning and Development Committee unanimously.

Steve Weir killed it.

He didn’t vote against it. There was no vote — Steve made sure of that. He and a
handful of allies threatened to talk until midnight, knowing the clock would run
out before a roll call could be called. Majority Leader Rojas pulled the bill.
The session ended. The bill died.

Why does Steve care so much about non-resident apartment owners? Funny you
should ask.

Steve sits on the board of the Connecticut Apartment Association — the trade
group for apartment owners. By CTAA’s own rule, his seat there has to be held in
the name of a company. He owns commercial real estate at 60 Village Place in
Glastonbury through an LLC, appraised at $1.69 million. In his own words on the
House floor that day, he is “a service provider to apartments.” In a 2024
candidate questionnaire, he said he spends “most of his time” on his commercial
property. None of this is on his official “About” page. None of it.

So when the General Assembly tried — for the first time in years — to give towns
real tools to deal with bad-acting absentee landlords, the representative for
Andover, Bolton, Glastonbury, Hebron, and Marlborough made sure they didn’t get
them. He killed it on behalf of a constituency that doesn’t live here, doesn’t
vote here, and doesn’t pay our taxes.

We haven’t even touched on his record on rent-increase notices, on housing code
liens, on the rights of mobile home park residents to buy their own parks.
That's a longer conversation -- and the full public-records picture is at
andoverct.info/reports/55th/weir-businesses.

For now, ask Steve who he was working for on May 6. And ask him why he didn’t
tell us.
